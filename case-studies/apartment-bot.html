Apartment Bot - How Rejection Can Lead to Creative Problem Solving

The First of Many No's

I remember the summer of 2021 well. After months of planning, I was devastated to hear that I couldn't study abroad due to changes in my major prerequisites. It was a huge blow, and I felt like I had hit a dead end. I was faced with the daunting task of finding a place to live off-campus during a housing crisis.

At the End of Every Line

I teamed up with three of my first-year roommates to find an apartment, but we quickly found out that it was going to be much harder than we thought. Every day, we called leasing offices and followed up on Zillow and Apartments.com posts, but we were always too late. The properties we were interested in were already gone, or we had to put our names on long waitlists in the hopes that availability would open up.

An Opportunity Presents Itself

But one day, during a call with a leasing agent, I decided to ask for advice. I wanted to know if there was anything we could do to improve our chances of finding a place to live. The leasing agent told me something that would change everything: Zillow and other listing sites usually don't update until a day or two after the apartment's personal website updates. If we wanted to stay ahead of the game, we would have to check the availability directly through their site.

Monty Python Makes The Holy Grail

I knew this was a great tip, but I was lazy and didn't want to spend all day checking each apartment complex's website. So, I had an idea. I decided to write a Python script that would do the heavy lifting for me. The script would create a WebDriver to go onto the apartment's website, scrape the live content, hash the content, save that hash locally to my computer, and then send a text message to me and my future roommates if the hash was different from the previous run. I set the script to run every thirty minutes, 24 hours a day.

A few days later, the bot found us an apartment that was close to a bus stop and well within our price range. We applied and were approved quickly. We couldn't believe our luck! We were thrilled to have found a place to live during such a difficult time.

Looking back, I realized that being rejected from studying abroad had led me down a path I never would have imagined. It had forced me to be creative and come up with a solution that not only solved my problem but also helped my friends.
